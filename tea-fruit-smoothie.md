# Fruit Smoothie with Tea Instead of Water

## Concept
A fruit smoothie that replaces the water (or ice) base with brewed tea, adding depth
of flavor, antioxidants, and subtle caffeine to a standard fruit smoothie. The tea
acts as both the liquid base and a flavor enhancer.

## The Recipe

### Ingredients
- 1 cup brewed tea (cooled or as ice cubes), e.g. green tea, hibiscus, or chai
- 1 cup mixed frozen fruit (mango, berries, pineapple, or banana)
- 1/2 cup fresh fruit (optional, for brightness)
- 1 tablespoon honey or maple syrup (optional)
- 1/2 teaspoon lemon juice (optional, brightens flavors)

### Instructions
1. Brew tea and let cool to room temperature, or freeze into ice cubes ahead of time.
2. Add tea to blender first, then add frozen fruit.
3. Blend on high for 30–60 seconds until smooth.
4. Taste and adjust sweetness with honey if desired.
5. Serve immediately.

## Tea Pairing Guide
| Tea | Pairs Best With | Flavor Profile |
|-----|----------------|----------------|
| Green tea | Mango, pineapple, kiwi | Fresh, grassy, light |
| Hibiscus | Berries, pomegranate, citrus | Tart, floral, vibrant |
| Chai | Banana, peach, apple | Spiced, warming, rich |
| Chamomile | Peach, apricot, honey | Floral, gentle, mellow |
| Matcha | Pineapple, banana, spinach | Earthy, bold, creamy |

## The Pitch
"Smoothies are stuck in a rut — protein powder, almond milk, same five ingredients.
Tea-based smoothies unlock a new flavor dimension: antioxidants from the tea stack
on top of the fruit's vitamins, caffeine replaces the need for a separate morning
coffee, and the flavor combinations are nearly infinite. It's a smarter smoothie
for people who already drink both tea and smoothies."

## Target User
Health-conscious adults who drink tea daily and make smoothies regularly but haven't
combined the two habits. Particularly appealing to people reducing dairy intake and
looking for a coffee alternative with less caffeine spike.

## Key Advantages Over Water-Based Smoothies
- Adds antioxidants (catechins in green tea, anthocyanins in hibiscus)
- Provides mild caffeine without a full coffee hit
- Dramatically expands flavor complexity with no added calories
- Brewed tea ice cubes prevent dilution as they melt
