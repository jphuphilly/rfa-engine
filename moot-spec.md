# Moot — Product Spec (App Store, 2024)

## The Product
Moot is a paid iOS app that presents users with five distinct AI personas who debate
the user's personal or professional dilemma and then reach a consensus verdict. The
user submits a decision they're wrestling with; five AI characters argue competing
perspectives; a final verdict synthesizes their debate into a recommended course of
action.

## The Five Personas
- **Visionary** — big-picture optimist who focuses on long-term upside
- **Analyst** — data-driven, probability-focused, risk-calibrated
- **Skeptic** — challenges assumptions, pokes holes, worst-case thinker
- **Mediator** — empathy-first, considers relationships and human factors
- **Architect** — systems thinker who focuses on execution and dependencies

## The Three Stages
1. **Debate** — All five personas argue their positions on the user's dilemma
2. **Deliberate** — Personas respond to each other's arguments, refine positions
3. **Decide** — A synthesized verdict is delivered with confidence level and rationale

## Monetization
- Paid iOS app (App Store)
- Currently running paid Facebook ads to drive downloads
- No freemium tier mentioned; premium positioning from the start

## Target Use Cases
- Consumer: "Should I quit my job?" / "Should I break up with my partner?"
- Professional: "Should I take this deal?" / "Should I hire this person?"

## Target User
Decision-fatigued professionals and self-improvement-oriented consumers who feel
paralyzed by important choices and want a structured outside perspective. People who
already pay for therapy, coaching, or journaling apps.

## The Pitch
"Every big decision deserves a real debate. Moot gives you five expert perspectives
arguing your dilemma from every angle — then delivers a verdict. Not generic advice.
Not a chatbot. A structured deliberation that forces your options into the light."

## Competitive Landscape
- Generic AI assistants (ChatGPT, Claude): single-perspective, no structure
- Therapy/coaching apps (BetterHelp, Coachable): human-powered, expensive, slow
- Decision-support tools (Pros/Cons lists, Notion templates): passive, no synthesis
- No direct multi-persona debate product with consumer verdict UX currently at scale

## Current Status (for RFA purposes)
Live on the App Store as a paid app. Running paid Facebook ads, implying CAC-driven
growth rather than organic/viral. The founder is spending money to acquire users
right now. Evaluate: is this a BUILD (keep scaling), PIVOT, or KILL?

## Key Open Questions
- What is the price point and retention/LTV?
- Is the verdict feature actually changing user behavior, or is it just entertaining?
- How does this defend against OpenAI/Apple shipping a "debate my decision" feature?
- Is Facebook the right channel for a decision-making app, or a sign of no organic pull?
